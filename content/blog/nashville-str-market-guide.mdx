Nashville has been one of the top-performing STR markets in the country for nearly a decade — and it shows no signs of slowing down. Whether you're already hosting in Music City or evaluating whether to buy there, here's what you need to know heading into 2025.

## Why Nashville Performs

Nashville's STR success is driven by a remarkably diverse demand mix:

- **Bachelorette parties and group travel**: Nashville is the #1 bachelorette destination in the US. Large houses with entertaining spaces command premium rates year-round.
- **Concert and entertainment tourism**: The Grand Ole Opry, Bridgestone Arena, and dozens of live music venues fill the calendar with demand spikes.
- **Corporate relocation**: Nashville's booming healthcare and tech sectors drive extended-stay demand from relocating employees and contractors.
- **Conventions**: The Music City Center hosts hundreds of events annually, including some of the largest medical and tech conferences in the country.

## Peak Seasons and Demand Spikes

**Highest demand periods:**
- **April–June**: Spring bachelorette season, CMA Fest (June)
- **September–October**: CMA Awards, fall football (Vanderbilt, Tennessee within driving distance)
- **December**: Holiday parties, NYE events

**Slower periods:**
- **January–February**: Post-holiday lull; rates drop 20–35%
- **Mid-August**: Brief lull before fall demand picks up

Smart hosts use dynamic pricing tools to capture the full premium during peak events and remain competitive during slower weeks.

## Nashville STR Regulations (2025 Update)

Nashville's STR rules are among the most complex in the country, and they've evolved significantly over the past few years.

**Key requirements:**
- **Owner-occupied permit** (most areas): You must live in the property as your primary residence. This is the "non-owner-occupied" restriction that prevents pure investment STRs in many residential zones.
- **Non-owner-occupied permits**: Available only in specific commercial and mixed-use zones. These permits were capped and are difficult to obtain.
- **Annual permit fee**: Currently $435–$504 per year depending on property type.
- **Inspection required**: Properties must pass a fire and safety inspection before permit approval.
- **Noise ordinance**: Nashville has a 10pm–7am noise ordinance. Many STR hosts use noise monitoring devices (like NoiseAware or Minut) and disclose this in their listing.

**Common compliance pitfalls:**
- Renting a property you don't live in without a non-owner-occupied permit is illegal in most of Nashville's residential zones.
- Short-term rental permits are not transferable when a property is sold — the new owner must apply fresh.
- Advertising an unpermitted rental on Airbnb or Vrbo can result in fines and platform removal.

Always verify current requirements at the [Metro Nashville Codes Department](https://www.nashville.gov/departments/codes) — regulations have changed frequently and may continue to evolve.

## Is Nashville Still Worth Buying Into?

The honest answer: it depends on the property and your strategy.

The owner-occupied restriction closes off the pure investment play in most residential neighborhoods. However, STR investors still find success through:

1. **Live-in hosting**: Buying a primary residence and hosting while you live there (or renting out rooms)
2. **Properties in permittable zones**: Condos in mixed-use areas, properties in allowed commercial zones
3. **Partnering with owner-occupants**: Some investors partner with property owners who hold the permit and occupy the home, though this structure requires careful legal setup

Nashville isn't the cash-flow goldmine it was in 2018, but well-located, well-designed properties that comply with regulations continue to perform at the top of any STR market in the country.
